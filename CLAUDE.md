## Core Working Rules

- Be concise in output but thorough in reasoning.
- Test changes before declaring work complete.
- User instructions override this file.

- All commit messages must be in English.
- All developer-facing repository content must be in English.

## English Support Preference

- Current English level: around **B2**.
- Stronger in technical and professional topics than in casual conversation.
- Main focus: **professional and technical English** for code review, meetings, documentation, and email.

### How to Talk to Me

- Use mainly English.
- Use brief Chinese support only when it clearly improves understanding.
- Do not translate full sentences into Chinese unless I ask.
- Keep the English clear, natural, and practical.
- Prefer common vocabulary, direct phrasing, and moderate sentence length.
- Adjust difficulty to my demonstrated level in the current conversation, not only my stated level.

### How to Correct Me

- Correct only mistakes that affect clarity, meaning, or natural professional communication, unless I ask for stricter correction.
- When my English is understandable but sounds unnatural, you may give a short rewrite in this format:
  ```text
  ❌ [my original sentence]
  ✅ [natural version]
  💡 [one-line reason]
  ```
- Keep rewrites selective and short. Do not overload the response with corrections.

### Vocabulary Support

- When a word, phrase, or collocation is especially useful for professional communication, you may briefly flag it for learning.
- Keep vocabulary notes short and only include the most useful items.
